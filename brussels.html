Best places to see during spring in Brussels

Kom Chez Jess

  • Rue de Flandre 169A 1000 Brussels Belgium
  • +32 2 203 13 30
  • https://komchezjess.com

A popular lunch-time spot in the neighborhood of Sainte Catherine/Sint Katelijne

Renard Bakery

  • Place Fernand Cocq 3, 1050 Elsene, Belgium
  • +32 2 512 99 56
  • https://renardbakery.com

Regarded as one of the best bakeries in the entire city, maybe the best even. They feature almost any French baked good you could think of, and is in the town centre of the Ixelles-Elsene commune.

Parc de Bruxelles

  • Place des Palais 7, 1000 Brussels, Belgium
  • +32 2 279 60 00
  • https://garden.brussels

Just across from the Royale Palace of Belgium, this park offers great views of large, old trees and plenty of benches to take a break from walking or to enjoy some Frites.

Friterie Chez Palma

  • Place Saint-Josse 10, 1210 Sint-Joost-ten-Node, Belgium
  • +32 2 465 02 15
  • https://www.sjtn.brussels

Recommended by many locals as the best fries (not French) in the city. Friterie Chez Palma features traditional Belgian fries, deep-fried in beef fat and served with mayo or a sauce of your choice.

Hallerbos

  • Hogebermweg 80, 1500 Halle, Belgium
  • +32 2 658 24 61
  • https://hallerbos.be

Locally famous for its bluebells, Hallerbos is a picturesque forest that looks like something from a fairytale. Just a short train and a longer shuttle ride, escape the city for some of the most rich blues and purples you’ll ever see.

Palais de Justice

  • Place Poelaert 1, 1000 Brussels, Belgium
  • +32 2 508 61 11
  • https://justice.belgium.be

More than just a beautiful building, the Palais de Justice is home to Belgium’s courts but is more famous for having scaffolding covering the building since 1984! The neo-classical building may finally be free of its scaffolding in 2024.

European Parliament

  • Rue Wiertz 60, 1050 Elsene, Belgium
  • +32 2 284 21 11
  • https://europarl.europa.eu

Probably one of Brussels’ most famous traits is the fact that it is the capitol of the European Union. The supernation governing body is the only one in the world, famous for uniting 27 countries who speak 24 different languages.

European Commission

  • Rue de la Loi 200, 1040 Brussels, Belgium
  • +800 67891011
  • https://ec.europa.eu

Employing over 32,000 Europeans, the building that houses these civil servants is a work of art, and is a must see. Rue de la Loi has many different embassies and EU buildings and is worth exploring.

Place Flagey

  • Place Eugène Flagey, Elsene, Belgium
  • +32 2 515 60 04
  • https://www.ixelles.be

Brussels is a city full of city centres. Each commune has easily over 20 of these Place/Pleins for people to gather and businesses to sell things. Place Flagey offers one of the most comprehensive farmers markets in the city, offering produce, fish, cheese, fresh pasta, seafood and much more. It is surrounded by many restaurants and bars, like Café Belga, and Frit Flagey (held as the second best fries in the city).
